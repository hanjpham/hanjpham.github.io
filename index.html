Welcome

I am a PhD candidate in Public Policy at Cornell University's Brooks School of Public Policy. My research integrates methods from health economics to study healthcare policy and market design. My work focuses on understanding how institutional structures and policy reforms shape healthcare delivery and access. I hold a bachelor's degree in Applied Mathematics with honors from Temple University.

Research

Job Market Paper

Funding Reform and Physician Supply: Evaluating Policy Implementation Using Choice Models

This paper examines how changes in healthcare funding mechanisms influence physician workforce distribution, with implications for access to care in underserved areas.
