Welcome

I am a PhD candidate in Public Policy at Cornell University's Brooks School of Public Policy, with research topics in Applied Microeconomics and Industrial Organization. My work integrates methods from economics and data analytics to study healthcare policy and market design.

Current Work

As a Graduate Research Assistant, I'm working on quantitative assessment of funding using choice models, analyzing $15B in federal funding and developing methods to estimate salary offers in the healthcare sector. I also study applications of marketing and accreditation data, such as evaluations of smoking cessation and teaching function of major hospitals.

Projects

Dissertation

Funding Reform and Physician Supply: Evaluating Policy Implementation Using Choice Models

Using data from Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services, American Medical Association, and Bureau of Labor Statistics, this research analyzes $15B in federal funding covering 80% of new U.S. doctors (2016-2019).

Dynamics of Choices among Doctors and Funding Implementation (from Manuscript)

Other Projects

E-cigarette Use and Smoking Cessation in Consumer Data

with Alan Mathios & Don Kenkel

Analysis of ~420K consumer records (2013-2019) to study cessation patterns among current smokers. Discrete survival analysis reveals evidence that commitment to switch can improve quit success despite limited impact of standalone e-cigarette use.

Teaching Function and Quality of Care

with Sean Nicholson & Colleen Carey

Investigating the components of hospital-based care team, with a focus on resident training. I use subtle disruptions and data from various sources to piece together an experiment separating the role of teaching function from other components.
